# Content Quality Rules

Two mandatory checks run on all content before it is presented to the user. Always run in this order.

---

## Priority 1: Sugarman Slippery Slope (Readability)

Every sentence should pull the reader into the next. Posts fail when blocks feel like isolated paragraphs rather than a continuous pull.

### Rules

1. **Connective tissue:** Each sentence creates a small question or anticipation that the next sentence answers. Use phrases like "The catch is that", "Which means", "But", "So", "That's why", "And that's only part of it"

2. **Bridge sentences between blocks:** Whenever the idea shifts (problem to discovery, revelation to features, stats to explanation), the new block must open with an explicit connective sentence. Do not hard-cut between ideas. Examples:
   - "And the speed is only part of it."
   - "Then the math hit me."
   - "But there's something else it does."

3. **Grouping:** Group 2-3 naturally connected sentences together rather than isolating every line

4. **Short sentence placement:** Short sentences are fine for punch, but they need a longer sentence before or after them to land

5. **Read-aloud test:** Read each block aloud. If it sounds like an isolated paragraph rather than a continuation, add a bridge

---

## Priority 2: Humanizer Pass (Remove AI Patterns)

Fix every instance of these patterns before the user sees the content:

### The 12-Point Checklist

1. **Negative parallelisms** — "No X. No Y. No Z." or "Not X. Not Y." Weave these into a sentence naturally instead of listing negatives

2. **"It's not X. It's Y." framing** — A subtle negative parallelism. Do not reframe by stating what something isn't. State what it IS directly

3. **Duplicate sentence subjects** — Avoid starting two consecutive sentences with the same subject+verb pattern (e.g., "I was still the middleman. I was doing this 40+ times a day."). Consolidate into one sentence

4. **Negative block pattern** — If a block opens with a negative construction AND closes with a negative phrase, that is a double flag. Rewrite either the lead or the ending to be positive

5. **Rule of three** — Do not force ideas into groups of three. Only use if genuinely listing three specific things

6. **Short fragments as sentence openers** — Full sentences only. No two-word or telegraphic fragments as openers. There is a difference between a short punchy sentence and a telegraphic fragment

7. **Em dash overuse** — Max 1 per post, max 2 per article. Use commas or rewrite instead

8. **Inline bolded headers with colons** — Story-driven sentences only. Do not start sections with "**Bold phrase:** explanation"

9. **AI vocabulary** — Remove these words: "game-changer", "seamless", "unlock", "supercharge", "revolutionary", "comprehensive", "robust", "cutting-edge", "vibrant", "stunning", "groundbreaking", "nestled", "renowned"

10. **Hedge language** — Remove: "think", "maybe", "might", "potentially", "pretty good", "works well", "in my opinion"

11. **Sycophantic tone** — No "Great question!", "Here's what you need to know!", "Absolutely!", or any similar phrases

12. **Same-length sentences back to back** — Vary rhythm. Same-length sentences feel algorithmic

### Writing Tone Rules

- Sentence length: 8-20 words baseline, occasional shorter punches for emphasis but not every other line
- Reads like someone talking, not like bullet points or marketing copy
- No AI hype words (see #9 above)
- No hedge words (see #10 above)
- Conversational and smooth, not choppy two-word fragments
